Is it cold up there?

It isn't that much different then on the ground. Generally 3 degrees cooler per 1000 feet. But we normally don't fly more than 200 feet so it won't feel like a big difference.

What should we wear?

Dressing in layers is recommended. In the morning, it's usually chilly when we begin and warms up when we're done. Easiest to add a layer or remove one.

When can we fly?

We fly in the two hour window after sunrise or before sunset.

What kind of fuel do you use?

Liquid Propane. We carry 30 to 40 gallons on board for a flight.

Do you need a license to fly balloons?

Yes! Its regulated by the Federal Aviation Administration. Getting a license requires written and oral test, many fours of flight training, and a flight test. As a hobby, you just need a Private Piltos License. For flying others, you need a Commercial Piltos License. Every 2 years, the license needs to be renewed.

Can I bring my kids?

Yes, we ask they be at least six years old.

Why do I have to get up so early?

We typically fly in the morning or sunset because that is when the wind is most predictable. Plus the early morning flights are the prettiest in our opinion.

How much wind do we need?

We need some but not too much. 2 to 4 miles per hour is ideal. 8 to 12 miles per hour gives us the ablity to cover more distance.

Where do we land?

Wherever the wind takes us! We usually have a good idea of where the wind will take us and generally where we may land. Anywhere free of powerline and lamp posts with lots of land!

Is it scary?

We sure don't think so! The basket is safe and secure. Any initial anxiety of fear reduces once you're up in the sky.

What if I freak out?

It is very rare that this happens, even those who are nervous end up loving it. But if that's the case we will land as soon as there is a suitable spot.

How long does a flight last?

Somewhere between 45 mins to an hour. We only cut it shorter if there's a great oppurtunity to land or bad weather.

How far do you go?

Depends on the speed of the wind, but anywhere from 3 to 12 miles.

What kind of weather do you need?

We need light winds. We can't fly in rain or storms. Even a storm a hundred miles away affects our local wind patterns. Let's say we watch the radar obsessively.
